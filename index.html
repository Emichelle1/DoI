Overview

The Department of the Interior (DOI) conserves and manages the Nation’s natural resources and cultural heritage for the benefit and enjoyment of the American people, provides scientific and other information about natural.

User Story

As a UI designer, you want to understand how people use the government agency navigation and website content so you can wireframe, design, prototype, and test a new website solution for desktop.

Tools Used: Miro, Adobe Xd, Trello, Slack, Zoom, Google Docs, Google Sheets, Google Forms, Adobe Photoshop, Adobe Illustrator

UI Design

Design Process

Scenario

You’ve just been hired as a UI designer for a government agency. The government agency wants to redesign and update their website. They have given you the next five weeks to execute the design process: research, define, ideate, prototype, and test a responsive website solution. By the end of this project, you will have a responsive website case study to place in your design portfolio.


Goal

My goal is to research the UI pain points people experience while interacting with a website. I’ll synthesize that research down into a user persona and UI analysis.


Research

Through our redesign, we were able to introduce new design elements from the U.S. Web Design Standards that help to make the site more intuitive. Our primary goals for the redesign included highlighting more social and engaging information and placing it right at your fingertips; making better use of real estate on our homepage; enhancing our site search to help you get to information faster; improving the site’s information architecture; and implementing responsive design across the entire site to provide a better viewing experience from any device.
